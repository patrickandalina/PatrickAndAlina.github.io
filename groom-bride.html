How We Met

Patrick Dancel

"Meeting Alina came at one of the most difficult times of my life. At the time, my mother had just been diagnosed with cancer (she's okay now) and I wasn't sure whether God was calling me back home to Florida. I wasn't sure what I wanted to do with my career. The only thing that was keeping me in California was me being the President of a Catholic young adult group in Orange County. After one of our retreats in August of 2017, Alina and I sat next to each other at a restuarant with the other young adults. I remember thinking the whole time, 'Wow, this girl is SO AMAZING! She's so beautiful and her personality is like so cool. I NEVER MEET GIRLS LIKE THIS AT THESE CATHOLIC YOUNG ADULT EVENTS, I GOTTA ASK HER OUT! But this doctor girl will never like a guy like me.' LOL. Once I finally had the courage to ask her out, she had to leave immediately to go visit one of her friends. I remember telling my roommate Ian to hold me accountable to asking her out on a date. Luckily I was able to connect with her on Facebook and from there we went on multiple dates and YEUH! God made it so clear that this was going to be the woman that I will strive for Heaven with, to raise a family with, and to endure the good and bad times with. I am so thankful to God, my family, and friends who have been a part of our journey as a couple. Please continue to pray for us as we eter into the sacrament of marriage."

Alina Marrone

"I too met Patrick at a very interesting time in my life! I was about to begin a busy year of residency and I was feeling lonely as my support group of PT school friends had mostly moved away and I was missing my family. I had just moved back to LA and I decided to go to a day retreat for a Catholic young adult group that I had learned about during my time in Texas to meet some new people. Patrick and I happened to sit next to each other at lunch and we hit it off. During that lunch we became facebook friends and he sent me a message asking me if I wanted to play Spikeball sometime. I'm horrible about checking social media and didn't see his message for two weeks! Anyway, we ended up exchanging numbers and texting for a while... Now if you ask Patrick, he will say that our 'first date' didn't really happen until later because he didn't actually ask me to go on this date. That first night we were texting, I was impatient and said "we should hang out sometime" trying to give him a hint that I liked him and wanted him to ask me out. We proceeded to meet up a few days later and have a fun night out in Pasadena eating sushi and dancing to my favorite cover band. At the end of our FIRST DATE, Patrick was very sweet and asked if he could take me out on a 'real date' and of course I said yes! We continued to see each other almost every week, despite the distance, and spent a lot of time on FaceTime. It was very different with Patrick. I felt like I could be open and share my true self and what I was struggling with. He pushed me to find truth and better myself. A few months later we both knew that this was a relationship given from God and what a wonderful day that was when we both realized we felt the same."
